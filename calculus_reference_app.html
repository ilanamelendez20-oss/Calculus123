1. Limits & Continuity

A limit describes the value a function approaches as the input gets closer to a specific value.

Definition:
\( \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \)

2. Derivatives

The derivative measures the instantaneous rate of change of a function.

Definition:
\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h} \)

Basic Rules

Power Rule: \( \frac{d}{dx} x^n = n x^{n-1} \)
Constant Multiple: \( \frac{d}{dx} [c \cdot f(x)] = c \cdot f'(x) \)
Sum / Difference: \( (f \pm g)' = f' \pm g' \)
Exponential: \( \frac{d}{dx} e^x = e^x \)

3. Chain Rule CRITICAL FOR AI

The Chain Rule is essential for understanding how neural networks learn through backpropagation.

Chain Rule:
\( \frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x) \)

Neural networks are made of stacked functions. The Chain Rule lets us calculate how a small change in the final output affects the weights in earlier layers.

4. Applications of Derivatives

Optimization Finding maximum and minimum values. This is the foundation of how neural networks minimize error during training.
Related Rates How one quantity changes when another quantity changes.

5. Integrals (Introduction)

Integration is the reverse of differentiation. It calculates accumulated change and areas under curves.

Power Rule: \( \int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \) (for \( n \neq -1 \))

6. Gradients & Optimization ESSENTIAL FOR AI

In machine learning we work with functions that have thousands of variables (weights). The gradient points in the direction of steepest increase.

Gradient:
\( \nabla f = \left( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_1}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_2}, \dots , \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_n} \right) \)

Gradient Descent (How Neural Networks Learn)

To minimize loss, we move in the opposite direction of the gradient:

New weights = Old weights − (learning rate × gradient)

This process is repeated thousands of times during training. This is why understanding gradients and the chain rule is so important for understanding how AI actually works.
